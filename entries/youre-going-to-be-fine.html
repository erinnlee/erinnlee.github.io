a continuation to neil chen's "You’re going to be fine, trust me"

big breaths. this one's for you.

To the new freshmen, how are you feeling physically, mentally, emotionally, spiritually? I don't know any of you yet, I bet there's a lot happening right now - orientation has come and gone, classes have just started. If you're one of the lucky ones, you'll have found your best friend(s) already. If you haven't, no fret - all it takes is a bit of time. Trust me, you're not the only person who feels this way. I'll believe in you until you can believe in yourself. Remember when getting into college seemed like the biggest thing ever? The best promise you can make to yourself is to stand up for who you are (or perhaps, who you'd like to be). Start to slowly figure out what makes sense to you (with the understanding that your decisions today should not limit you tomorrow). Deep breaths. I am so excited for your adventures - after all, you are in the city the rest of the world dreams of living in.

To the newly appointed sophomores and juniors, how are you feeling physically, mentally, emotionally, spiritually? Does it feel weird in this new role? Do you feel more at home? I bet the worries you had in your first years have somewhat simmered - I might even bet that there are some new worries that have settled in. This idea of "real life" might feel a bit more ominious this year - try not to give it too much thought. Because think about it: aren't you living life now? Isn't it real? I hope that you've been able to at least find one person you've looked up to in your first years. Do you think you have capacity now to pay it forward? Maybe to someone who's just arrived and are currently standing in the shoes you once did?

To the seniors, how are you feeling physically, mentally, emotionally, spiritually? At this point, it always seems easier to think about what we could have done differently. We can't change the past though - only look towards the future. Have you been able to take time and think about who you were last year, two years ago, your first year? Do you think you've changed (the answer ought to be yes), and if so, do these changes shock you? Do you remember when you first met your best friend(s)? Take a second to think about how far you've come both personally and collectively - it's the least you can do for yourself. I hope you more empathetic towards yourself and to the world. I hope you're proud of who you are / who you're becoming. This idea of growth ought not to be finite - in fact, I'm keen to think that we're just getting started.

We're always the hardest on ourselves, but I ask, maybe for a day, could we collectively try to treat ourselves like we'd treat our dear friends? Maybe we can try for 2 days after that, a week? How about we make it a norm? Personally, I think it's quite funny to think that as I'm growing older, my mind is getting younger. I'm surprised at how whimsical and wonderfully happy the world can be if we look to putting good energy inside ourselves and in turn, outwards to the rest of the universe.

So, in conclusion, you're exactly where you're supposed to be. 3 deep breaths. Like Neil said, "chances are, you're actually doing great." And I echo him when he says, "but if you're not, trust me - you're going to be fine."
